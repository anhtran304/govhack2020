The Story

The Port Arthur penal settlement began life as a small timber station in 1830 and quickly grew in importance within the colonies. Shipbuilding was introduced on a large scale to Port Arthur as a way of providing selected convicts with a useful skill they could take with them once freed. The 1853 cessation of transportation resulted in fewer transportees arriving at the station. However, the 1850s and 1860s were years of remarkable activity, that aimed to make the station economically sustainable.
